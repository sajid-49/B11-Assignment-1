Bangabandhu Satellite-1 is Bangladesh's first geostationary communications satellite, launched on May 12, 2018, from the Kennedy Space Center in Florida, USA, aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket. Named after the nation's founding leader, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, the satellite marked Bangladesh as the 57th country to own a satellite in space.

Positioned at 119.1° East longitude, approximately 35,786 kilometers above the equator, Bangabandhu-1 provides a range of services, including broadcasting, telecommunications, and data communications. It is equipped with 26 Ku-band and 14 C-band transponders, offering coverage over Bangladesh and neighboring regions such as India, Nepal, Bhutan, Sri Lanka, the Philippines, and Indonesia.
