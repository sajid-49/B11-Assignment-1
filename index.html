Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi economist, entrepreneur, and visionary widely recognized for his groundbreaking work in poverty alleviation and economic empowerment. Born on June 28, 1940, in Chittagong, Bangladesh, he revolutionized financial systems for the poor through the concept of microcredit and the establishment of Grameen Bank in 1983. The bank provides small, collateral-free loans to impoverished people, primarily women, enabling them to start small businesses and achieve financial independence.
